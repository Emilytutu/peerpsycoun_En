Self-reporting Inventory 

Self-reporting Inventory, also known as Symptom Checklist 90 (SCL-90), sometimes called Hopkin's Symptom Checklist (HSCL, compiled earlier than SCL-90 by the same author, of which the earliest version was compiled in 1954) was compiled in 1975 by L.R. Derogatis. The 90-item scale contains a wide range of psychiatric symptomatology, from sensation, emotion, thinking, consciousness, behavior to habits, interpersonal relationships, diet and sleep, etc., and uses 10 factors to reflect 10 aspects of psychological symptoms respectively.

Rating Period:It can be rated for a specific time, usually for a period of one week.

Rating Methods:Each of its items is rated on a scale of 1 to 5, as described in the table below.

Note:Below is a list of 90 test items that some people may have problems with. Please read each one carefully. Then, according to your actual feeling within the last week, select and click the appropriate answer. Please be careful not to skip any items.
